What is Graffiti


Any person walking around an urban citys has seen the colorful,expressive, illegal "scar in a community" that is graffiti. Although
many consider the spray-painted pieces a nuisance, graffiti has been gaining recognition from the art world more and more
as a legitimate form of art.When most people think of graffiti, they imagine "tags," or a stylized writing of a person's name.
While tags are probably the most popular forms, graffiti art is much more than that. It can mean a colorful mural with a message
of diversity or rascial stigma ,or it could depict police brutaulity

The Decline of Art and Graffiti

Modern graffiti didn't start until 1960 in philadelphia.Shortly after the death of Charlie Parker (or "Bird") in 1955, graffiti began appearing around New York city and New York City Subway cars with the words "Bird Lives"but it took about 15 years for the graffiti craze to hit New York City.In New york city the font in graffitti changed from "Bubble" to "Wild" and "Crazy" and names such as DONDI, Lady Pink, Zephyr, Julio 204, STAY HIGH 149, PHASE 2 went from Bubble to wild. In the 1980 graffitit was declared an act of vandalism and the clean movement was passed through.The clean movement was making sure that most graffiti ridden places, such as train, were cleaned or either replace it.With the last graffiti-covered train out of service by 1989 and With subway trains being increasingly inaccessible, many graffiti artists had to resort to new ways to express themselves. Rooftops became the new canvas for some graffiti artists.
